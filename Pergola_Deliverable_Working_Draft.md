## 01 · Executive Summary

Day54 was engaged by Pergola Management for a focused 2–3 week operations and technology audit, centered on how the team uses Rent Manager (RM) — identifying underused features, workflow inconsistencies, and opportunities to operate more efficiently without adding complexity or cost.

Pergola is a well-run, intentionally right-sized company of ~600 units across Minneapolis/St. Paul and Rochester. The business is not seeking growth; the goal is to run current operations better, with greater visibility and less reliance on individual knowledge and manual workarounds.

**The through-line:** Pergola runs on **cost data without operational data**. Across findings the pattern repeats — the team can see what was spent or that something happened, but not the operational detail needed to manage it proactively. The data that goes missing is consistently the data that depends on a person entering it at the moment of an action; auto-captured data is already complete.

**Audit lanes:** RM feature adoption · two-location workflow consistency · maintenance closeout & turnover documentation · utility trend visibility.

### Software spend, reconciled
The kickoff framed spend as ~$18K/yr and "half-used." LCS (Rent Manager) bills ~$14.8K/yr directly ($14.1K core + ~$0.7K VPO printed notices). The remainder is the integration partners — AvidXchange (A/P) and Zego/PayLease (tenant payments) — billed separately. AvidXchange runs ~$700–800/mo (figure to be confirmed against an actual invoice); critically, that spend is the paid **AvidInvoice** piece — the **AvidPay** payment rail is no-cost for RM users above a payment-volume threshold, so part of the bill may be recoverable without any system change (see Finding 12).

Two carry-forwards: (1) the subscription spans ~7 separately-numbered RM accounts, the licensing footprint of the two-location structure (F07); (2) RM's AI/automation add-ons (Orion AI, Smart Bills, Bank Sync) are not currently licensed — the baseline if AI tooling is revisited later.

## 03 · Findings

Priority key: **Quick Win** (team can do directly) · **High Impact** (significant, may need guidance) · **Strategic** (longer-term/architectural).

### Finding 01 — Metered Utilities Module Completely Unused · *High Impact*
- **Observed:** Services → Metered Utilities entirely empty — zero configuration of providers, meter types, readings, or RUBS posting.
- **What it means:** RM is fully equipped to capture consumption (gallons, kWh, therms) by property, trend it, and bill via RUBS. None configured. Utility management happens in PayLease (RUBS) and AvidXchange (vendor bills), with zero consumption data in RM.
- **The gap:** No way to trend usage, normalize to occupancy, compare YoY, or flag a mid-month anomaly. The business sees what it paid, not what it used.
- **Why it matters:** Rochester Heights shows ~60% YoY gas-cost increase with no way to attribute it. A past water-pressure/valve event should have been a detectable consumption spike — it wasn't, because data was never captured. Brian named utility trend visibility as a wanted metric.
- **Recommendation:** Phase 1 (quick win) configure providers + meter types, log monthly readings → enables native reports. Phase 2 (build) pull consumption + occupancy via Open Access/API into an external reporting layer with threshold alerts. Faster path: consumption already exists in Zego/PayLease (utility recovery performance tab) — explore importing rather than only manual entry. Prereq: confirm API/Open Access status.

### Finding 02 — Residents Cannot View Signed Leases in the Tenant Portal · *Quick Win*
- PM must email each signed lease manually; residents can't see it in the portal.
- Likely a Blue Moon eSignatures post-back setting that was never enabled. Check the setting, turn it on, confirm with RM support if needed.

### Finding 03 — Unit-Transfer Lease Re-Issuance Requires Owner Workaround · *Quick Win*
- When a resident moves to another unit, RM pulls the old unit's info; the PM can't complete it and an owner has to "trick" the system.
- Flag the intra-portfolio transfer scenario to the RM rep; document the supported steps so the PM can handle it independently.

### Finding 04 — Maintenance Logging Is a Strength; the Real Gaps Are a Stale Open-Ticket Tail and After-Hours Intake · *Quick Win* · **[Updated Jun 26]**
- **What the live review found:** Service Manager holds **19,383 issues** at ~13h average age — RM is the genuine intake/resolution point for the bulk of logged work, across channels and the full portfolio. Maintenance adoption is a **strength**, not a gap.
- **Reassurance [Updated Jun 26]:** The reality is *better than assumed at kickoff*. The expectation that closeouts mostly happen on paper door-tags or not at all is contradicted by the field — techs write short notes on the service order and close it out, and sampled closed tickets carry resolution notes. The remaining closeout gap is **photos and parts/chargeback** (treated in F11), not blank tickets.
- **The narrow, real gaps:** (1) a stale open-ticket tail (~71 open; a few back to 2024; several untriaged); (2) urgent/after-hours intake that still arrives by text/call and may be worked before a ticket exists.
- **Owner motivations to fold in [Updated Jun 26]:** Brian framed closeout discipline around three business reasons beyond documentation:
  - **Liability** — untracked "while-you're-here" extras create exposure (his example: a tech hanging a flat-screen TV that could break).
  - **Performance metric** — tracked extras distinguish a genuinely productive tech from one being "led around" by tenants; useful for annual reviews.
  - **Enforcement mechanism (Brian's own idea)** — gate the next work order / parts request on closing out the current one.
- **Recommendation:** Reframe for Brian as reassurance — logging works at scale; the items are small and targeted. Add a monthly triage of the open tail; a one-line after-the-fact ticket rule for urgent/after-hours work; and consider Brian's gate-the-next-ticket mechanism to drive closeout depth (notes/photos/parts).
- *Neutral structural note [Updated Jun 26]:* logging discipline varies across the team; the owners-level practice (categorized history on every interaction) is the standard worth generalizing. (No individuals named.)

### Finding 05 — Leasing Funnel Is Invisible — Prospects Enter RM Only at Application · *High Impact*
- Inquiries come via Zillow/Apartments.com; prospects enter RM only at formal application. Everything before that (inquiry volume, response time, showings, no-shows, dropped leads) is invisible. Confirmed across PMs — multiple keep separate Google Sheets for showing logs.
- Live review: prospect records are "born at application"; Lead Information (source, agent) is empty even on those. Back half of funnel (application → screening → approval → lease → move-in) works; **front half** is the gap.
- **Recommendation — assemble the funnel from where each stage already lives:** top of funnel from Zillow/Apartments dashboards (don't rebuild records, that imports noise); middle (contacted → showing → applied) is the only part worth a lightweight tracker, and only once a lead is *real* (source-tagged); bottom already works in RM.

### Finding 06 — Utility Expenses Coded Differently on Each Side of the Business · *High Impact*
- Rochester uses clean dedicated utility accounts; Minneapolis/St. Paul posts to a catch-all "Gas & Electric (joint service)" — gas and electric can't be separated.
- GL-level expression of the two-location drift (two legacy portfolios merged in 2008). Undercuts the visibility Brian wants and is a prerequisite to fix before any F01 reporting build.
- **Recommendation:** Standardize per-utility accounts across both locations; re-map the catch-all going forward; sequence before the F01 Phase 2 build.

### Finding 07 — Two-Location Split: A Daily Maintenance-Visibility Problem First, an Account-Fragmentation/Consolidation Question Second · *Strategic* · **[Updated Jun 26 — reframed]**
- **Origin [Updated Jun 26]:** The consolidation question did not start as a cost issue — it started with **maintenance**. Per Joe at kickoff, a tech logged into one RM location **cannot see the other location's work orders on the app** and must switch locations. That is why work gets relayed by text instead (e.g., Bloomington work orders forwarded between techs). This is a daily operational friction, and it is the original reason the two-location structure came under review. It directly manufactures some of the text-message intake counted against F04.
- **Structural backdrop:** The subscription carries ~7 separately-numbered RM accounts (6 desktop RM12 + 1 RM Online) — the licensing footprint of running two legacy portfolios in one platform. This is the same drift seen in workflow (this finding) and GL coding (F06).
- **Open question (not yet resolved):** Can the two locations be merged without losing history, and would consolidation reduce the per-account count? (LCS/Caitlin: merge is feasible but partial-history-only and quoted as a paid project; blocked on Brian's trial-balance-scope decision.)
- **Recommendation:** Treat consolidation as the structural fix to a real daily friction (maintenance visibility), not merely a licensing saving. Put merge feasibility + licensing impact to the RM rep; don't recommend a merge until the data-loss question is answered.

### Finding 08 — Printed Resident Notices (VPO) Could Shift to Portal/Email · *Quick Win*
- ~$58/mo (~$700/yr) for ~49 mailed notices/mo. Actively used, not waste. Shift notices to residents who already have email/portal; keep mail for legally-required service. Ties to F02 (more portal adoption → more avoidable mail).

### Finding 09 — Turnover Tracked in External Excel; Make Ready Board Owned but Not Yet Activated · *High Impact*
- Off-system turnover confirmed across locations — PMs run the process on personal Excel/Google sheets, manually copy-pasting from RM; no shared visibility, record sits on one device.
- RM's Make Ready module is owned and capable (each item is a full Service Issue; templates attach to properties). Live review: taxonomy populated, one "Rochester Heights" template exists, but the board was never put into service.
- **Root cause (confirmed):** the audit login lacked add/update privileges across Service Manager / Metered Utilities (explicit RM error: "Insufficient privileges to add or update data"). A Jun 24 privilege grant restored the *select* layer; two narrow add-level rights still fail (add a Make Ready Action; enable Meter Estimates). Access problem, not absent configuration. (Same Metered Utilities gap also blocks F01.)
- **Recommendation:** Grant the two remaining add-level rights and retry; escalate to RM rep with the exact error if a full-privilege login still fails. Then build a turnover template (8-step draft ready), validate on one live unit (confirm multi-unit view + automatic tenant pull), then extend. Faster parallel path: a native RM turnover report meets the core roster/reporting need even before the board is live.
- *rmInspection note:* owned (RM Online bundle) but dormant; move-out inspection currently exists only as a single make-ready action line — activating it structures the inspection record.

### Finding 10 — Communication Logging Is Partial: Calls Auto-Link but Go Un-Notated, Individual Email Stays in Outlook · *Quick Win*
- rmVoIP linking works as designed (verified). Net observations: calls auto-log but carry no conversation notation; outbound RM email logs, but individual two-way threads live in Outlook; one genuine defect — the "Unlinked Calls" view spins indefinitely (support ticket).
- **Recommendation:** light habit of notating calls; log/BCC critical tenant email into RM; open the support ticket.
- *Neutral structural note [Updated Jun 26]:* logging completeness depends on manual entry at the moment of action — the same through-line; practice varies across the team, with the owners-level discipline as the standard. (No individuals named.)

### Finding 11 — Parts and Procurement: Cost Is Visible, Operational Detail and Chargeback Are Not · *High Impact* · **[Updated Jun 26 — corrected]**
- **What's actually true about visibility [Updated Jun 26]:** Cost attribution by **property** and by **owner** already **exists**. Mechanism: PO codes per property at point of purchase (Menards on contractor pricing; Home Depot on a separate card); statements show POs; multi-property buys get split codes (e.g., 889/908) that Chad divides out. The **owner split differs by side**: Joe's side uses credit cards (for points) with Joe/Mark/Chris purchases commingled on one monthly statement that Chad allocates line-by-line; Brian's side runs through AvidXchange + check reimbursement; Darcy (Rochester) also uses a card. **Returns usually carry no PO**, so they leak from attribution.
- **The real gaps [Updated Jun 26]:**
  1. **Operational granularity** one layer down — per job/work-order (for chargeback), per unit, per tech, turnover-vs-routine, category volume — lives in the financial/GL layer (Chad's manual reconciliation), not the operational/work-order layer.
  2. **Manual burden:** visibility is achieved through significant by-hand labor by one person (Chad splitting a commingled card statement).
  - So: they **can** budget per property/owner; what they **can't** see is *why* a building is expensive (per-unit/per-issue drivers), and they can't tie parts to a job for chargeback, catch shrinkage/duplicate buying, or negotiate volume pricing.
- **Procurement is unstaffed by design [Updated Jun 26]:** the dedicated maintenance-manager role was cut, so techs absorbed ordering/scheduling/coordination. The fix is tooling/process, **not** a new hire and **not** a PM chore. (Chris's "manager pre-walks units to stock/order" is aspirational, not current practice.)
- **What RM offers, unused:** each Service Issue has a Work Orders line-item grid (Item, Qty, Cost, **Sale Price**, Total) — the native parts + chargeback mechanism — empty on sampled tickets. Full native inventory suite (catalog, reorder report, physical inventory worksheet, POs) is empty; the ~13 catalog items are a chargeback price book, not stock.
- **Parts-in-notes nuance [Updated Jun 26]:** it's not "no record" — techs (e.g., Otis) write parts as **prose in the closeout note**; it's an *unstructured* record that can't drive chargeback math or attribution because it's not in the Work Orders grid.
- **Recommendation — right-sized, lowest-overhead first:**
  1. Consolidate to 2–3 suppliers with online ordering + delivery (kills trips + middleman markup; zero inventory overhead).
  2. Par-level visual list per building (faster-than-counting glance sheet; not a maintained RM quantity).
  3. Log parts on the work order for chargeback (recoverable tenant-damage money; per-job attribution at near-zero friction; give-away = attach the receipt photo).
  4. Full RM inventory only if the logging habit proves sustainable (auto-decrement via work-order items); for a 3-tech/600-unit shop it may cost more admin time than it saves.
  - *Minor inventory nuance [Updated Jun 26]:* salvaged good used parts (e.g., Mark's practice) carry no SKU/cost and would sit outside any reorder logic if inventory is scoped.
- **Company-card spend / Ramp:** now its own finding — see **Finding 13**.

### Finding 12 — Accounts Payable: AvidXchange Works but Is Under-Optimized on Cost; Orion/Smart Bills Is a Quote-Worthy Alternative, Not a Presumed Switch · *High Impact* · **[Added Jun 26]**
- **Current state:** Paperless A/P via AvidXchange since ~2019; Chad is sole admin. Approval has simplified to mostly Chad (only large/unique/disputed route to Joe/Brian). Payments via ACH autopay, paper check (Avid now charges to mail; ~2-week lag), and virtual card (small rebate, immediate cash-out, some vendor pushback). Cost ~$700–800/mo (figure soft — to confirm against an invoice).
- **Pain points:** AI miscategorization (account-number dash bug, "I"-as-"1" misreads, **duplicate invoices paid twice**, statements read as invoices, stylized fonts); exception vendors bypass it entirely (Menards, Home Depot, mortgage, Xcel paper); one-invoice-per-PDF intake; approval-queue latency → late fees.
- **The cost is being misread:** AvidXchange = **AvidPay** (payment rail — no-cost for RM users if ≥50% of payments route through it; ~$4–6K/yr savings) + **AvidInvoice** (the paid capture/approval automation). So the monthly spend is likely AvidInvoice + check-mailing fees, and **part may be recoverable with no migration** by confirming the 50% AvidPay threshold and shifting checks → virtual card/ACH.
- **Orion/Smart Bills (RM native AI A/P)** replicates Avid (AI capture, vendor email intake, ACH/check/VCC) and adds Smart Receipts (mobile), Smart Check Scanning, and Bank Sync + electronic reconciliation. **Three gates:** (1) requires **RM Plus/Premium tier** — Pergola is on a legacy itemized/custom invoice plan, not one of RM's set tiers (which is itself why Orion isn't available), so adopting it means migrating onto a Plus/Premium plan and repricing the whole RM subscription, possibly above the Avid line it replaces (the make-or-break unknown); (2) **single-reviewer approval only** (Avid does multi-tier) — probably fine given Chad-mostly approval, but confirm no dual Joe+Brian requirement; (3) RM positions Smart Bills for low-moderate volume/in-house, Avid for med-high/outsourced — need the real invoice count.
- **Recommendation (sequenced):**
  1. *Quick win, no migration:* confirm AvidPay 50% status, shift checks → virtual card/ACH, right-size the bill.
  2. *Decision:* get two quotes side by side — (RM Plus/Premium + Smart Bills) vs. (current RM + optimized Avid). Switch only if the Orion total ≤ optimized-Avid total **and** single-reviewer approval is sufficient.
  3. *Ask RM:* can Smart Bills (capture) coexist with no-cost AvidPay (payment rail)? If yes, a best-of-both option — drop paid AvidInvoice, keep the free payment rail + vendor network.
- **Effort:** low for the optimization quick win; the switch is a scoped, quote-gated decision, not a toggle. High migration bar for a lean shop ~6–7 years into Avid.

### Finding 13 — Company-Card Spend Runs on Manual Reconciliation; a Controlled Expense-Card Platform Would Automate It · *High Impact* · **[Added Jun 26]**
- **The pain:** Joe's side uses credit cards (for points); one monthly statement **commingles Joe + Mark + Chris** purchases. Chad reconciles line-by-line, categorizes, splits by entity (Rochester/Crystal/Brian/Joe), and cuts Joe reimbursement checks. Darcy (Rochester) also has a card. Cards are effectively open — no category controls; one-offs (gas, truck repair, oil change, office snacks) mix with parts. Brian's side avoids cards (Avid + checks). So spend control and categorization are a manual, single-person burden, and the cards carry no guardrails.
- **Recommendation:** a controlled expense-card platform (e.g., Ramp — free; revenue from merchant interchange) issues cards with category/merchant restrictions (e.g., hardware-stores-only for techs), mandatory receipt-photo capture with AI categorization, weekly approval, and automatic split/export by entity. It eliminates the manual statement-splitting, adds the spend guardrails the owners would want, and unlocks online ordering for the techs (ties F11). Per-user controls let Joe keep his points-style card while constraining the techs.
- **Caveat:** Joe is the gatekeeper (it's his points habit) — adoption hinges on his buy-in. Net cost ≈ zero.
- **Effort:** low–medium — account setup, card issuance, and a light approval habit; no RM change.

**Operational notes (outside RM scope):** mixed appliance brands/models within a property add maintenance time; no defined turnover/remodel scope at acquisition drives "round one, then round two" rework.
